Important Notice

CareerFit was developed based on research findings on horizontal career mismatch among college graduates. The platform is intended for educational guidance, career awareness, and self-reflection purposes only.

About the study

Understanding horizontal career mismatch in a practical, student-friendly way

CareerFit translates the thesis findings into a guided web experience: students can understand why mismatch happens, see how labor-market structure shapes choices, explore related career options, and answer the full survey in one session.

Ecological factors

What students can see in the labor market

Visibility of job openings, local opportunities, commuting realities, and family context can influence whether graduates accept work outside their degree field.

Psychological factors

How students respond to pressure and opportunity

Confidence, resilience, work self-efficacy, and career adaptability shape how ready students feel to pursue degree-aligned work.

Labor-market structure

Mismatch is not only a personal decision

The thesis found that perceived job availability was the only significant predictor of horizontal career mismatch. This supports a structural explanation: graduates may accept unrelated work when field-specific openings are limited or difficult to access.

Evidence base

What authors show about field mismatch

Montt (2015) and Somers, Cabus, and Maassen van den Brink (2018) describe horizontal mismatch as work outside one's field of study. Robst (2007) and Beduwe and Giret (2011) link mismatch with wage, productivity, and satisfaction costs, while Melchor (2022, 2024) connects Philippine mismatch to education-labor market alignment.

HCM Horizontal Career Mismatch measures degree-to-job misalignment.
Structure Limited degree-aligned openings can push graduates toward unrelated jobs.
Guidance Students can compare aligned job options before or after answering the survey.

About the research

Short answers to common student questions

A small FAQ helps students understand what HCM means, why the survey matters, and what happens after they submit.

What is HCM?

HCM means Horizontal Career Mismatch. It describes a situation where a person’s current work is not closely aligned with the degree they completed.

Who can take this survey?

The survey is open to three respondent types: college students currently enrolled in a degree program, incoming college students (SHS graduates or those about to enroll), and working respondents who have graduated or are currently employed.

Why am I being asked these questions?

The survey looks at mismatch together with job availability, salary perception, location and commuting realities, family influence, career adaptability, resilience, and work self-efficacy.

I am an incoming college student — how should I answer?

Answer based on your expectations and perceptions about your intended degree and future career. You do not need current work experience — the survey captures how you see your future situation right now.

I am already working — how should I answer?

Reflect on your current job relative to your completed degree, training, or work background. The working survey uses workplace wording so the items match employed or graduated respondents.

Who sees my answers?

Your answers are saved within the survey system connected to this project so your results can be generated and your submission can be stored as part of the survey process.

Can I view my results right away?

Yes. After a successful submission, the site immediately shows your full scores, interpretations, and a visual summary of the eight dimensions.
